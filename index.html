What is Vote Buying?

Vote buying is the act of giving money, goods, or favors in exchange for a person's vote. It is illegal and violates the principles of free and fair elections.

It happens when candidates or political groups try to control voters by offering money, food packs, gifts, or other benefits in return for their vote.

Why Vote Buying is Wrong

  • Destroys the fairness of elections
  • Allows corrupt leaders to win
  • Ignores the true choice of the people
  • Weakens trust in government
  • Encourages a culture of bribery and dishonesty

Effects on Society

Vote buying harms communities in many ways:

  • Corrupt leaders steal public funds to recover their “expenses”
  • Poor quality services like roads, healthcare, and education
  • Communities stay poor due to bad governance
  • Citizens lose faith in democracy and elections

Laws & Penalties

In the Philippines, vote buying is a serious crime under the Omnibus Election Code.

  • Up to 6 years imprisonment
  • Disqualification from holding public office
  • Loss of voting rights
  • Fines and legal charges

Both the buyer and the voter who accepts money can be punished.

How Citizens Can Help

  • Refuse money or gifts from political candidates
  • Report vote buying to COMELEC or local authorities
  • Educate others about why vote buying is harmful
  • Choose leaders based on honesty and platform
  • Participate in civic discussions and community programs
